Your closet is personal

Privacy, in
plain language.

Effective date · July 22, 2026

Closette is a wardrobe companion for iPhone. This policy explains what information the app uses, where it is stored, and the choices you have. We aim to collect only what is needed to make the product work.

What you add to Closette

Closette can store wardrobe photos, clothing descriptions, style tags, outfit plans, wear history, preferences, and other content you choose to create. This information is used to provide closet organization, search, outfit suggestions, calendar planning, and related features.

Where your data lives

Your wardrobe is designed to stay on your device and, when you enable iCloud, in Apple’s iCloud/CloudKit services associated with your Apple Account. Apple’s terms and privacy practices also apply to those services. Closette does not sell your wardrobe data.

Sign in with Apple

Sign in with Apple is optional. If you use it, Apple may provide an identifier and the name or relay email you choose to share. Closette uses this information to associate your account and sign-in state with the app.

Photos, location, and weather

Photo access is used only when you choose to import or select wardrobe images. Location access is optional and is used to provide local weather context for styling. You can use a manual location instead, or turn access off in iOS Settings.

Purchases

Subscriptions and lifetime purchases are processed by Apple through StoreKit. Closette receives transaction and entitlement information needed to unlock purchased features, restore purchases, and recognize subscription status. Closette does not receive your payment card number.

Analytics and diagnostics

The current portfolio build uses local-only event and diagnostic storage with no configured Aptabase account key. These local records support product quality and can be removed through account/data controls. If a future release introduces a remote analytics service, this policy will be updated before that change ships.

Sharing and third parties

When you choose to share an outfit or image, iOS presents the share sheet and you decide which app or person receives it. We do not sell personal information or use wardrobe photos for advertising.

Retention and deletion

You can remove individual wardrobe content in the app. You can also use the account deletion controls to delete local closet data and request deletion of associated cloud data where applicable. Some purchase records may be retained by Apple for billing, fraud prevention, or legal requirements.

Children

Closette is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Contact

Questions about privacy or a data request? Email feedback@closette.app.

This page describes the current portfolio build and may be updated as Closette moves toward release.
